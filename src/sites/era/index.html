BlakeStar Coin Swap Information 100 BLAS = 1 ERA

A coin swap is when a cryptocurrency moves from one blockchain to another. As the coins on one blockchain may be incompatible with the new one, it becomes necessary to initiate a swap so no users lose out on their coins.

A new team recently took over the BlakeStar project and formed a foundation with the aim of creating real world usage for the cryptocurrency.

Steps if you wallet holder:
  1. Send you your funds to your ERA Wallet address before dates end;
  2. Open Swap tab and send funds to generated for you ERA address;
  3. System will automatically swaps your old BLAS coins to new ERA. Old coins will be burned.
Steps if you exchange holder:
  1. Hold your funds before exchanges announce swap dates;
  2. On these dates swap will be triggered automatically and you will get new ERA coins.

Important! After coin swap BLAS network will be fully abandoned by the dev team and all supported exchanges will delist BLAS network. All unused generated coins will be public burned in one day after coin swap procedure will ends

ERA Network / Node Specifications

Blake2s itself is a high speed, optimized cryptographic hashing algorithm used for Proof-of-Work and Proof-of-Stake hashes in BlakeStar for performance with low load. It uses Proof-of-Work, but unlike Bitcoin it is configured to transition to Proof-of-Stake, when small numbers of coins will be issued yearly for staking (10% yearly).

The advantage of transitioning to Proof-of-Stake is to avoid the centralized mining pools that may eventually threaten Bitcoin with a 51% attack. Most Bitcoin mining has been centralized in China, which is not necessarily a good thing for the currency. Proof-of-Stake is known to discourage centralized mining pools.
